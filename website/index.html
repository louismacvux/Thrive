Thrive

Our vision:

"Why settle for survival, when you can be thriving?"

Thrive will provide quick and easy access to multi-faceted tools and information that will assist the consumer in optimizing their health, well-being and performance. The application aims to strike a balance between providing a range of both personalized and generic features that are instrumental for the consumer to excel and thrive.

Target audience:

Thrive is primarily designed for individuals concerned with their health and productivity, thus looking to track their health information altogether and improve their overall health. Thrive is also a perfect fit for most fitness enthusiasts as it will allow them to access vital tools that are necessary in physical performance and maintaining / improving ones energy levels.

Major Functionalities

WELLNESS CALCULATOR

A compact calculator that can help them determine their current BMI and maintenance calories needed to maintain current bodily functions.

BLOOD OPTIMIZER

Suggestions on improving imbalances amongst the most common blood health markers based on their specific gender.

WORKOUT SUGGESTION

Thrive offers the user access to customized workout plans that are based on the users personal goals and their specific gender.

MEAL PLANS

Healthy meal plan ideas that the user can choose from according to their maintanence calories.

Why use Thrive?

The answer lies in the convenience and integration of different health components that Thrive brings together for the user. Think of Thrive as your personal health goals assistant and tracker.

As opposed to narrowing in on a specific niche or problem like most other applications, Thrive takes pride in adapting a holistic approach to addressing different health domains.

Combining the basic elements that are the foundation to a healthy and thriving life, presented in a simple and easy to use interface for users across most age demographics.

Postmortem

At the end of iteration 2, we experienced some minor shortcomings. There were some solid violations in our code that needed to be refactored and we had issues with the integration tests not being complete due to not having a working real database in place to support the tests. During our iteration 3, we had the database implementation as our high priority task and completed it and had the team review the code to avoid code smells.

• What went wrong in the development process?
We had some problems in iteration 1, where we had documentation requirements that were not satisfied and very weak test suites.

• What went right in the development process?
Transitioning from our first iteration, we decided to have more frequent team meetings with improved communication to check up on our progress and make sure all the delegated tasks were being met as planned.

• What would you do differently, if you had the chance to start over?
We would narrow down our focus on the quality of the features rather than the quantity and plan the coding thoroughly before jumping in to reduce technical debt.

• What did you learn about team or large project development? What will you start doing or keep doing?
If everyone on the team is willing to make things work, team work can be highly efficient as compared to working individually. We will continue to improve the organizational skills and communication which are the keys to a team project.
